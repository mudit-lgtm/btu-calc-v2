What is a BTU?

BTU — British Thermal Unit
The amount of heat needed to raise 1 pound of water by 1°F. In HVAC, BTU/hr measures how much heating or cooling an appliance delivers per hour.

Key BTU Conversions

  • ❄️
    1 Ton of AC = 12,000 BTU/hr Standard US measurement for air conditioner capacity
  • ⚡
    1 kW = 3,412 BTU/hr Converts electric power to heat energy for comparisons
  • 🌡️
    1 Watt = 3.412 BTU/hr Used to calculate server room and electronics heat loads
  • 🔥
    1 Therm = 100,000 BTU Used for natural gas billing and furnace sizing

How Many BTU Do I Need?

The answer depends on room size, climate zone, and insulation. Use these starting rules:

  • ❄️
    Cooling (AC): ~20 BTU per sq ft 500 sq ft room → ~10,000 BTU. Adjust for climate and insulation.
  • 🔥
    Heating (Furnace): 30–60 BTU per sq ft Hot climates: 30 BTU/sq ft. Very cold climates: 60 BTU/sq ft.
  • 💨
    Mini Split: Same as AC + 10–20% for heating Size up slightly in heating-dominant climates for efficiency.
  • 🌡️
    Radiator: 60–100 watts (205–341 BTU) per m² UK standard for panel radiators at ΔT50 system temperature.

BTU Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

Direct answers to the most common BTU questions.

How many BTU do I need per square foot?

Cooling (AC): approximately 20 BTU per square foot as a starting point.

Heating (furnace): depends on climate zone:

  • Hot climates (FL, TX, AZ): 25–35 BTU/sq ft
  • Mixed climates (Midwest, SE): 40–45 BTU/sq ft
  • Cold climates (Northeast): 50–55 BTU/sq ft
  • Very cold (Canada, Mountain): 55–60 BTU/sq ft

Use our Heating BTU Calculator for a climate-adjusted result.

How do I calculate BTU for a room?

Basic formula: Room sq ft × 20 = Base BTU (cooling)

Then adjust for:

  • Ceiling above 8 ft: multiply by (height ÷ 8)
  • Sunny/south-facing room: add 10%
  • Shaded/north-facing room: subtract 10%
  • More than 2 occupants: add 600 BTU each
  • Kitchen: add 4,000 BTU
  • Poor insulation: add 15–25%

Full guide: How to Calculate BTU →

What size AC do I need for 1,000 sq ft?

A 1,000 sq ft space typically needs 18,000–21,000 BTU (1.5 tons) in a moderate climate.

  • Hot climate (TX, FL): up to 24,000 BTU (2 tons)
  • Cool climate (PNW, NE): 15,000–18,000 BTU
  • High ceilings (10 ft+): add 10–25%
  • Poor insulation: add 15–25%

Get your exact result: AC BTU Calculator →

How many BTU is 1 ton of AC?

1 ton of air conditioning = 12,000 BTU/hr.

  • 1.5 tons = 18,000 BTU/hr
  • 2 tons = 24,000 BTU/hr
  • 2.5 tons = 30,000 BTU/hr
  • 3 tons = 36,000 BTU/hr
  • 4 tons = 48,000 BTU/hr
  • 5 tons = 60,000 BTU/hr

Convert instantly: BTU to Tons Calculator →

How to convert BTU to watts or kW?

BTU/hr → Watts: multiply by 0.2931 (12,000 BTU = 3,517 W)

BTU/hr → kW: multiply by 0.000293 (12,000 BTU = 3.52 kW)

Watts → BTU/hr: multiply by 3.412 (1,000 W = 3,412 BTU/hr)

kW → BTU/hr: multiply by 3,412 (1 kW = 3,412 BTU/hr)

What BTU mini split do I need for 500 sq ft?

A 500 sq ft room typically needs a 12,000 BTU (1-ton) mini split for cooling in a moderate climate.

For heating-dominant climates (Zone 4–6), size up to 15,000–18,000 BTU to maintain efficiency at low outdoor temperatures.

What size furnace do I need for a 2,000 sq ft home?

A 2,000 sq ft home typically needs a 60,000–100,000 BTU furnace:

  • Warm climate (Zone 2–3): 60,000 BTU (60K)
  • Mixed climate (Zone 4): 80,000 BTU (80K)
  • Cold climate (Zone 5–6): 100,000 BTU (100K)

Heating BTU Calculator →

How do I calculate BTU for a garage or server room?

Garage: An uninsulated 2-car garage (400–500 sq ft) needs 24,000–36,000 BTU for heating in cold climates.

Server room: Add up total server wattage, then multiply by 3.412 to get BTU/hr heat output that must be removed.
