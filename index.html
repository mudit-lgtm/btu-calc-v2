What is BTU?

What is a BTU?

A BTU, or British Thermal Unit, measures how much heat energy is needed to raise one pound of water by one degree Fahrenheit. In HVAC work, BTU usually means how much heating or cooling output a system can deliver in one hour. If you want the long-form explanation, formulas, and examples, read our complete guide on how to calculate BTU.

Why BTU sizing matters

Choosing the wrong BTU output can create expensive comfort problems. Oversized cooling equipment cycles too quickly, misses humidity control, and uses more electricity. Undersized equipment runs nonstop and still fails to hold setpoint during peak weather. The same logic applies to heating: too much furnace capacity wastes fuel and can make rooms feel uneven, while too little capacity leaves cold spots and longer runtimes. Frequently Asked Questions

What is a BTU calculator?

A BTU calculator estimates the heating or cooling load of a room or building. Inputs like area, volume, insulation, climate, and occupancy help determine the recommended output range.

How many BTU do I need for air conditioning?

A quick rule of thumb is 20 BTU per square foot for cooling, then adjust for sun, ceiling height, occupancy, insulation, and kitchens. Use the dedicated AC BTU calculator for a more realistic answer.

How many BTU per square foot do I need for heating?

Heating loads usually fall between 20 and 50 BTU per square foot depending on climate and insulation. Very cold climates and drafty homes need more output than newer, well-insulated homes.

Can I size a mini split online?

Yes. A mini split calculator is useful for estimating single-zone and multi-zone loads, especially when you compare bedroom, garage, kitchen, and living room usage patterns.

Does BTUCalc work for contractors and engineers?

Yes. The website is designed for homeowners and pros who need planning-grade sizing fast. For permitting or final equipment selection, follow up with Manual J, manufacturer data, and site conditions.
