How We Calculate AC BTU

Our AC BTU calculator starts with the common planning rule of roughly 20 BTU per square foot for a standard room. That baseline is then refined using the same real-world factors HVAC installers watch during field sizing: climate severity, ceiling height, direct sun exposure, internal gains from people, kitchen heat, and insulation quality. A hot, sunny room with poor insulation needs noticeably more cooling than a shaded room of the same floor area. Taller ceilings also matter because the air volume increases beyond a typical eight-foot room. When more than two people use the room regularly, the calculator adds sensible heat for each extra person. Kitchen-adjacent spaces get an additional allowance because cooking appliances and warm surfaces increase the load. After applying each multiplier and adjustment, the final total is rounded to the nearest 500 BTU so the answer lines up with actual AC and mini split equipment sizes. That gives you a practical shopping target rather than a theoretical load with no real product match.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many BTU do I need for AC?

Most rooms start with a 20 BTU per square foot rule, then adjust upward or downward for local climate, occupancy, ceiling height, sun exposure, insulation, and kitchen heat.

How many square feet will 12000 BTU cool?

A 12000 BTU air conditioner commonly covers about 450 to 550 square feet in average conditions, but hot climates or sunny rooms can reduce that effective coverage.

Is 8000 BTU enough for a bedroom?

For many average bedrooms, yes, but a sunny upper-floor room or a large bedroom may need more than 8000 BTU to stay comfortable during peak summer weather.

What if my AC is too large?

An oversized AC can cool the air too quickly, short cycle, waste energy, and leave humidity behind, which often makes the room feel cool but damp.

Should I add BTU for kitchen heat and people?

Yes. Extra people and kitchen equipment add real internal heat gains, so they should be included when estimating the correct cooling capacity.
