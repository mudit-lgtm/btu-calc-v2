How We Calculate Appliance BTU

Appliance sizing depends on use case. Gas fireplaces and wood stoves are sized against room load and appliance efficiency. Outdoor heaters focus on area and target temperature rise. Pools and spas use water volume and temperature lift because heating water requires much more energy than conditioning air. Boiler sizing follows a heating-load approach using square footage, system type, and climate.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I size a gas fireplace?

Estimate room load based on area, ceiling height, insulation, and desired heating contribution, then divide by efficiency to determine appliance input size.

How many BTU does a wood stove need?

Wood stove sizing depends on room volume, climate, insulation, and desired burn cycle. Oversized stoves can overheat small spaces and reduce burn quality.

What size pool heater do I need?

Pool heaters are often sized from 100,000 to 400,000+ BTU depending on gallons, desired temperature rise, wind exposure, and how quickly you want recovery.

How do I calculate hot tub BTU?

Use spa water volume and temperature lift, then adjust for outdoor conditions and whether the spa uses an insulated cover when not in use.
