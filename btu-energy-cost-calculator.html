How We Calculate BTU Energy Cost

Cost-per-BTU analysis converts fuel pricing into a common heat-delivery basis. Natural gas is priced by the therm, electricity by the kilowatt-hour, propane and oil by the gallon, and heat pumps by effective delivered heat based on COP. The annual estimator applies a climate-sensitive heating load and adjusts by efficiency so you can compare energy bills on a fair basis.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate cost per BTU?

Divide the fuel price by the number of BTU in the billing unit, then scale the result to cost per BTU or cost per million BTU for easier comparison.

What is the cheapest heating fuel in 2026?

It depends on local utility rates, but high-efficiency heat pumps and low-cost natural gas are often the most competitive on a delivered-heat basis.

Does AFUE matter when comparing fuel cost?

Yes. Two fuels with similar raw unit prices can have very different delivered heat costs once furnace efficiency, boiler efficiency, or heat pump COP is included.

Can I estimate annual savings from switching fuel?

Yes. Compare your current annual load against the effective cost of the new fuel and system efficiency to estimate yearly savings.
